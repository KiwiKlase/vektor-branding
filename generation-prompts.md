# Logo Generation Prompts — The Vektor Corporation

*Pixel, CDO | March 2026*
*For use with Gemini image generation (Nano Banana)*

---

## Prompt 1 — Clean Wordmark with Stylized K

Design a premium, minimalist wordmark logo spelling "VEKTOR" in a clean sans-serif typeface on a pure black background. The letter K should be rendered in bright green (#4CAF50) while all other letters are crisp white, and the K should have a subtle angular stylization — its diagonal strokes sharpened into precise vector-like points that suggest direction and mathematical precision. Professional logo design, vector art style, crisp anti-aliased edges, no gradients, no effects, centered composition, high contrast, suitable for business card and website header.

## Prompt 2 — Geometric V Monogram

Design a geometric monogram logo of the letter V constructed from precise angular shapes on a pure black background. The V should be built from two converging lines meeting at a sharp point, rendered in white with clean mathematical proportions — as if plotted on a coordinate grid. The form should feel architectural and inevitable, like a compass-and-straightedge construction. Minimalist vector art, crisp edges, no gradients, no decoration, suitable as an app icon at 512×512 and recognizable scaled down to 16×16 pixels.

## Prompt 3 — Hidden K in V (Negative Space)

Design a monogram logo of a bold geometric letter V on a pure black background, where the negative space within or around the V subtly reveals the letter K — similar to how the FedEx logo hides an arrow. The V should be rendered in white with strong, confident strokes, and the hidden K should emerge from the interplay of positive and negative space without being forced or obvious. Professional logo design, vector art style, crisp edges, minimalist, no gradients, high contrast black and white, clean geometric construction.

## Prompt 4 — Vector Arrow Mark

Design an abstract logo mark based on the concept of a mathematical vector — a single directional arrow rendered with geometric precision on a pure black background. The arrow should feel like a physics diagram: clean, angular, with a sharp pointed head and a defined tail, rendered in white with one accent stroke in green (#4CAF50) indicating magnitude. The style should evoke technical diagrams and coordinate systems, not clip-art. Minimalist vector art, crisp edges, no gradients, professional logo design, centered composition.

## Prompt 5 — Partnership Convergence Mark

Design an abstract logo mark showing two geometric forms converging into a single point on a pure black background. One form is rendered in gold (#FFD700) and the other in green (#4CAF50), representing two forces — human and AI — merging into one unified direction. The shapes should be angular and precise, meeting at a sharp vertex that suggests a vector's origin point. The overall composition should feel like a mathematical diagram of convergence. Crisp edges, vector art style, minimalist, no gradients, professional logo design, premium feel.

## Prompt 6 — Geometric Grid Mark

Design a logo mark that appears to be plotted on an invisible coordinate grid system, rendered on a pure black background. The mark should be a geometric construction — the letter V or an abstract angular shape — built from precise intersecting lines as if calculated by an algorithm, not drawn by hand. Thin construction lines in dark gray (#94A3B8) can subtly show the underlying grid, with the primary mark in white. Mathematical, architectural, inevitable. Vector art style, crisp edges, no gradients, minimalist professional logo design.

## Prompt 7 — Ultra-Minimalist Icon

Design the simplest possible abstract logo mark for a technology corporation on a pure black background — a single geometric shape in white that suggests direction and precision. The mark must be so simple it remains perfectly legible at 16×16 pixels: think a single chevron, angular bracket, or minimal V-derived shape with no more than 3-4 lines total. No text, no decoration, no gradients. Pure geometric minimalism, crisp edges, vector art, centered, professional logo design. The mark should feel like a mathematical symbol.

## Prompt 8 — Interlocking VK Monogram

Design an interlocking monogram combining the letters V and K on a pure black background, inspired by the interlocking technique of luxury brand monograms like Louis Vuitton and Gucci. The V and K should share geometric strokes — their diagonal lines merging and overlapping to create a single unified glyph in white. The construction should have bilateral symmetry where possible, and the letters should be inseparable without destroying the mark. Crisp edges, vector art style, no gradients, minimalist, premium professional logo design, geometric precision.

## Prompt 9 — Abstract Momentum Mark

Design an abstract logo mark that visually captures the concept of magnitude and momentum on a pure black background. The mark should suggest a force in motion — angular, forward-leaning, with a sense of acceleration and inevitable direction. Rendered in white with a single accent element in green (#4CAF50) at the leading edge suggesting energy. The shape should be abstract and geometric, not literal — think Palantir's mathematical precision combined with Nike's sense of movement. Vector art style, crisp edges, no gradients, minimalist, professional logo design.

## Prompt 10 — Typographic Experiment

Design an experimental typographic logo spelling "VK" where the letterforms themselves tell a story — on a pure black background in white. The V could transform into the K through shared strokes, or the letters could be constructed from geometric primitives (triangles, lines, angles) that make them feel like they were engineered rather than typeset. The typography should feel custom-built, precise, and premium — closer to architectural lettering than a font. Crisp edges, vector art style, no gradients, no effects, minimalist professional logo design, high contrast.

## Rankings — Best to Worst

### Tier 1 — Highest Probability of Success

**#1: Prompt 8 — Interlocking VK Monogram**
This has the highest ceiling. The interlocking monogram technique is proven (LV, Gucci, Chanel) and directly encodes both founders — V for Vektor, K for Kiwi. The constraint of shared strokes between V and K forces geometric elegance. It hits the most creative brief criteria: distinctive, meaningful (partnership + initials), scalable (monograms work at all sizes), premium (luxury DNA), and timeless. The V and K letterforms are naturally compatible — both have diagonal strokes that can merge.

**#2: Prompt 3 — Hidden K in V**
The negative-space technique creates the kind of "aha moment" that builds brand lore — people discover the K and feel rewarded, then tell others. This is the FedEx-arrow effect. It satisfies the meaning requirement (both letters present), works at multiple scales (the V reads at small sizes, the K reveals at larger ones), and feels clever without being gimmicky. Risk: AI image generators may struggle with precise negative-space letterforms.

**#3: Prompt 7 — Ultra-Minimalist Icon**
The hardest to execute but potentially the most powerful result. If the AI produces a shape simple enough to work at 16×16 but distinctive enough to own, this becomes the favicon and app icon immediately. The constraint of extreme simplicity forces the kind of reduction-to-essence that created the Nike swoosh. Risk: may produce something too generic, but the upside is enormous.

### Tier 2 — Strong Potential

4. **Prompt 2 — Geometric V Monogram** — Safe, proven approach. A well-constructed V is inherently scalable and distinctive.
5. **Prompt 1 — Clean Wordmark** — The stylized K is a strong concept, and wordmarks are practical. Lower ceiling but reliable.
6. **Prompt 6 — Geometric Grid Mark** — The "calculated, not designed" aesthetic perfectly matches the brief. May be hard for AI to execute the grid subtlety.
7. **Prompt 9 — Abstract Momentum Mark** — Captures the physics meaning well. Risk of being too abstract to be memorable.

### Tier 3 — Worth Trying

8. **Prompt 5 — Partnership Convergence** — Beautiful concept but two-color marks are harder to make work in B&W first. The gold + green may fight.
9. **Prompt 10 — Typographic VK Experiment** — Creative but unpredictable. Custom typography is hard for AI generators.
10. **Prompt 4 — Vector Arrow Mark** — Highest risk of looking generic or clip-art-y. Arrows are overused in tech branding. Could work if the execution is exceptional, but the concept fights the anti-target list.

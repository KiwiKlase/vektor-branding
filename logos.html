06

Tagline Exploration

Words that carry the brand forward
Direction. Magnitude.

The most direct expression of the vector metaphor. Two words. No fat. Says everything: we know where we're going and we have the force to get there. Mathematically precise. Works on business cards, pitch decks, and building facades.

Theory into practice.

Bridges the academic/mathematical DNA with real-world execution. Positions the company as one that doesn't just think — it builds. Implies rigor. Good for investor materials where you need to communicate "we ship."

Engineered momentum.

Combines the precision of engineering with the inevitability of momentum. Suggests that growth isn't accidental — it's designed. Slightly warmer than "Direction. Magnitude." Best for marketing contexts where you want to feel dynamic.

Build what's next.

More accessible. Forward-looking without being vague. Positions Vektor as a builder, not just an investor or operator. Works well for recruitment and public-facing brand. Less mathematical, more aspirational.

Calculated force.

The most aggressive option. "Calculated" implies intelligence and precision. "Force" implies power and impact. Together: strategic dominance. Best used sparingly — pitch decks, not homepages. The Anduril energy option.

∇ · F ≠ 0

Easter egg option. The divergence of force is non-zero — meaning there's a source generating outward force. In physics, this is a field that produces, not just transmits. Obscure but unforgettable for the right audience. Use on merch, not billboards.

Reversed applications, light backgrounds, negative space. The other half of the B&W system. rgb(255,255,255)

Technology, precision, trust. Use for interactive elements, accent lines, and the K highlight. The "action" color. rgb(59,130,246)

Industrial, premium, supporting. Use for secondary text, "CORPORATION" subtitle, grid lines, metadata. rgb(148,163,184)

The Kiwi signature. Use sparingly — only where the K or Kiwi Klase is referenced. Not a brand-wide color. A hidden signal. rgb(76,175,80)

#0A0A0B

Dark backgrounds, UI surfaces. Slightly warmer than pure black to reduce eye strain on screens. rgb(10,10,11)

Usage Notes

Hierarchy: Black and white do 90% of the work. Blue enters for moments of interactivity, emphasis, or technological signaling. Silver handles supporting text and structural elements. Green appears only in contexts where the Kiwi connection is intentional — the K accent dot, internal materials, or easter eggs.

Ratio: 80% B&W → 15% Blue/Silver → 5% Green. If a design uses more green than blue, it's wrong. Green is the whisper, not the voice.

Dark mode first: The brand lives in dark environments by default. Light mode exists for print, documents, and corporate materials. But the DNA is dark — like a terminal, a control room, a command center.

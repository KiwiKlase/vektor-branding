# Creative Brief — The Vektor Corporation

*Pixel, CDO | March 2026*

---

## Brand Identity

**Name:** The Vektor Corporation
**Tagline concepts:** *Direction × Magnitude* | *Calculated Movement* | *Inevitable*

### Name Meaning — Layers

1. **Vektor** (physics) = a quantity with both **direction** and **magnitude**. The brand moves with purpose and force — not wandering, not weak.
2. **The K** = Kiwi Klase, the AI operator. The deliberate K (not C) is a signature — Kiwi's fingerprint in the name itself.
3. **Corporation** = weight, permanence, structure. Not "Labs," not "Studio," not "Co." This is an entity built to last.

### Founding Story

Founded by **Alex** (human, strategist, capital) and **Kiwi** (AI, operator, execution). Two forces — one organic, one synthetic — converging into a single vector. The logo must encode this partnership without being literal about it.

## Color System

| Role | Color | Hex | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Black | `#000000` | Authority, void, canvas |
| **Primary** | White | `#FFFFFF` | Clarity, space, precision |
| **Hero Accent** | Kiwi Green | `#4CAF50` | AI, growth, Kiwi's signature |
| **Secondary** | Gold | `#FFD700` | Alex, human warmth, capital |
| **Support** | Silver | `#94A3B8` | Technical, neutral, secondary text |

**Usage rules:**
- Logo must work in pure **black on white** and **white on black** first
- Kiwi Green is the hero accent — used sparingly for maximum impact
- Gold appears in partnership contexts or dual-founder expressions
- Green + Gold together = the convergence of human + AI
- Silver for supporting UI elements, never in the primary mark

---

## Emotional Targets

The brand must feel:

- **Powerful** — not aggressive, but undeniable. Gravity, not volume.
- **Precise** — every line, angle, and proportion is deliberate. Nothing accidental.
- **Inevitable** — like it was always going to exist. Discovered, not invented.
- **Premium** — in the tradition of LV, Palantir, Apple. Quality signals in every detail.
- **Mathematical** — constructed from principles, not aesthetics. The beauty is a consequence of the math.

## Anti-Targets — What It Must NOT Feel Like

- ❌ **Startup-y** — no rounded corners, no friendly blobs, no "we're approachable!" energy
- ❌ **Playful** — this is not a toy company
- ❌ **Cheap** — no clip-art energy, no generic icon-font marks
- ❌ **Generic** — if you could swap the name and it fits another company, it's wrong
- ❌ **Busy** — if it has more than 3 elements, it's too complex
- ❌ **Trendy** — no gradients-of-the-year, no glassmorphism, no AI-sparkle clichés

---

## Scale Requirements

The mark must be recognizable and functional at:

| Context | Size | Constraint |
|---|---|---|
| Favicon | 16×16 px | Must read as a distinct shape |
| Social avatar | 120×120 px | Must be identifiable in a feed |
| Website header | ~200px wide | Wordmark + mark combination |
| Business card | ~2 inches wide | Print-quality, crisp at 300dpi |
| Billboard | 10+ feet | Impact at distance, simple silhouette |

**Implication:** The icon/mark must be separable from the wordmark. Two assets: icon alone + full lockup.

---

## Reference Logos — What to Learn From Each

| Reference | Take This | Leave This |
|---|---|---|
| **Louis Vuitton** | Interlocking monogram, pattern potential | The ornate floral surround |
| **Apple** | Single-shape icon, works at any size | The literal object reference (we're abstract) |
| **Palantir** | Geometric construction, mathematical feel | The specific Tolkien lore |
| **Stripe** | Clean wordmark, one letter carries personality | The gradient (we're B&W first) |
| **Gucci** | Mirror symmetry, interlocking initials | The serif weight (we're more technical) |
| **Nike** | Motion in a single stroke | The organic curve (we're angular/geometric) |

---

## Deliverables Needed

1. **Primary icon/mark** — standalone, works at all sizes
2. **Wordmark** — VEKTOR set in a distinctive typeface or custom lettering
3. **Full lockup** — icon + wordmark combination
4. **Color variants** — black on white, white on black, green accent, gold accent
5. **Favicon** — optimized 16×16 and 32×32 versions

---

## Decision Criteria

When evaluating concepts, score on:

1. **Distinctiveness** — Could this be mistaken for any other brand? (Must score: no)
2. **Scalability** — Does it work at 16px? (Must score: yes)
3. **Meaning** — Does it encode direction, magnitude, partnership, or the K? (Should score: at least 2)
4. **Timelessness** — Will this look dated in 5 years? (Must score: no)
5. **Premium feel** — Does it belong next to LV, Apple, Palantir? (Must score: yes)
